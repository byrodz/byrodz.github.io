so many tools
so little time.

The concept of this project was to create an interactive augmented reality poster centered around the theme/metaphor "so many tools, so little time." I decided to explore this theme through the lens of a phobia, specifically acrophobia (fear of heights). To bring this vision to life, I illustrated a city viewed from above and used the AR animation to enhance the sense of vertigo.

This project was developed using Adobe Aero, allowing the users to engage with the virtual poster by scanning the QR code and clicking in the poster directly. For those who prefer a preview, a demonstration video is provided below to showcase the augmented reality experience.

breathing.

Together with the TEDxGENEVA team, I was invited to develop the graphic identity for the event "Breathing" taking place in Geneva on December 1, 2023. Although the project was not fully completed due to personal reasons, I was able to develop these incredible illustrations/concept posters for the event's identity.
The version that came closest to completion represents the concept of breathing in a creative and genuine way. Fluid and soft elements with small plants representing nature circulate through two floating doors, the entrance and the exit. To complete the piece, the presence of all these elements creates a shadow of the silhouette of lungs, the main organs responsible for breathing.
This entire piece was developed based on the following excerpt:

“Breathing seems at the same time so normal and then so frivolous and fragile. An act of life quasi unconscious and, yet, particularly meaningful: all life, somehow, breathes.

We breathe in joy and laughter.
We breathe in between tears and sadness.
We breathe in anger and despair.
We breathe in a leap of hope and light.
We breathe in life and out of it.

Couldn’t the very act of breathing inspire us, when thinking of forests, water, air, life ecosystems? Or mushrooms, animals, microbes and viruses, life in them? Who are the actors, the agents of change that create breathable environments? New spaces to breathe? New infrastructures to thrive upon ? What can we learn from them, how can we contribute to their work? Let’s take a moment to look around us and find them.”
- TEDxGeneva , Edition BREATHING, 2023 -

logo.

The idea behind this project was to develop my personal logo, something that would represent me distinctly in a professional context. To achieve this, I used the initials of my name, "R" and "C" arranged in a way that resembles a cube. This geometric approach not only highlights the initials but also conveys a sense of structure and multidimensionality.

My aim was not only to achieve visually satisfying aesthetics but also to create something that could be easily integrated across various platforms, colors and mediums. By not limiting the logo to a specific color palette, it becomes adaptable to different backgrounds and uses. This flexibility also ensures that my brand remains consistent and recognizable, regardless of the context.

rolling loud animation concept.

This is purely a conceptual animation for the Rolling Loud festival logo, allowing me to express myself freely and creatively regarding the festival's branding.

The animation begins with three characters dancing, each with different shapes and sizes to represent inclusivity and convey that the festival is for everyone. After about two seconds, the character’s heads are used as floating elements to form the logo. These shapes also symbolize that the fans are the heart of the event, emphasizing that the festival exists because of them. Finally, the three shapes come together at the end of the animation to stay true to the original logo.

posters collection.

For this project, I had the opportunity to express myself about a phase I am currently going through, the fight against cancer, depicted in a collection of 3 posters. The goal was to represent this struggle in 3 distinct phases, each corresponding to a specific date and the emotions of the moment expressed through an object.

Phase 1 "Headphones"
This phase represents the onset of symptoms, a very early and ignorant stage, marked by the date December 19, 2023. I considered it to be nothing too worrisome or significant. At that time, I also believed it to be something temporary, so I represented it with headphones, recalling moments when we use them during a walk, and it's just us and the music, ignoring everything happening around us.

Phase 2 "Rubik's Cube"
In this phase, the goal was to represent the moment I received the diagnosis on January 16, 2024. Here, I tried to convey the feeling of confusion, almost as if I were lost, using the Rubik's Cube, an object where, despite believing we are in control, one mistake can completely throw us off.
Spoiler Alert! In this poster, I also included a small puzzle written in the algorithm used to identify the different solution phases of a Rubik's Cube. Once solved, you encounter the famous Konami code (Up, Up, Down, Down, Left, Right, Left, Right, B, A). This cheat code, created in 1986 for Konami video games, gives the player more power and helps them win. I included it in the puzzle because, during this phase, I had many people who helped me psychologically, giving me the strength to continue.

Phase 3 "Rubber Duck"
Finally, this phase represents the beginning of chemotherapy treatments on February 11 2024, a stage characterized by innocence and vulnerability, where power and control are no longer in my hands, but in those of my doctors and caregivers. I chose a rubber duck to symbolize childhood, a being whose decisions are mostly made or influenced by others.

These 3 pieces also incorporate an augmented reality animation. To provide more context, I added a soundtrack composed by me, full of details that I found relevant and that reference the treatments.
